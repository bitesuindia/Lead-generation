Amazon Review Rating — Frequently Asked Questions

How does Amazon calculate the star rating?

Amazon uses a weighted average — not a simple mean — where recent reviews and verified purchases carry more weight. The base formula is: New Rating = (Current Rating × Review Count + New Stars) ÷ (Count + 1). This calculator uses that formula to show you exactly how many reviews you need.

How many 5-star reviews to reach 4.5 stars on Amazon?

It depends entirely on your current count and rating. A product at 4.2★ with 50 reviews needs ~30 five-star reviews. The same target from 4.0★ with 200 reviews needs ~100. Use the calculator above — enter your exact numbers and get your answer in seconds.

Does Amazon star rating affect PPC and ACoS?

Directly. A product with 4.5★ typically converts 2–3× better than the same product at 3.5★. Better conversion means Amazon's algorithm charges less per click and ranks your Sponsored Products higher at the same bid. Improving your rating is often the highest-ROI action before scaling ad spend — more than lowering bids.

What Amazon star rating is considered good?

4.5★ and above — best-in-category, highest conversion, eligible for "Amazon's Choice" badge. 4.0★–4.4★ — competitive, good CTR. 3.5★–3.9★ — below average, noticeably lower conversion. Below 3.5★ — Amazon may suppress the listing from search results entirely.

How do I get more Amazon reviews fast (compliant methods)?

1. Use the "Request a Review" button in Seller Central within 4–30 days of delivery. 2. Enrol in Amazon Vine for new ASINs (requires Brand Registry). 3. Optimise your insert cards to direct buyers to leave feedback. 4. Improve product quality to reduce negative reviews — a single 1★ can take 5–10 five-star reviews to neutralise.
