## 1. The Competitive Landscape (What We Found)

### Adyogi (adyogi.com) — The Market Leader to Beat

**What they are:** Gurugram-based SaaS + managed services hybrid. Meta Business Partner, Google Premier Partner, Amazon Advanced Partner. Built around their own ad automation platform.

**Their numbers:** $150M+ ad spend managed, 350+ brands, 5M+ catalog size.

**Their client profile:** Enterprise fashion brands — Pepe Jeans, Levi's, Manyavar, Raymond, Puma, Reebok. Big names. Big budgets.

**Their real weakness:**
- Minimum spend threshold likely ₹5L+/month — mid-market sellers CAN'T afford them
- Fashion-category obsession — they don't serve general merchandise, electronics, home, FMCG sellers
- No real UK Amazon expertise — India-first, limited cross-border play
- Platform-first pitch — their tool IS the product; human strategy is secondary
- Flipkart/Myntra barely mentioned — India multi-marketplace is not their strength
- Expensive — enterprise pricing for enterprise brands

**Their biggest gap:** The ₹5L–₹50L/month GMV seller who needs hands-on expertise, multi-marketplace coverage, and human account management — not a dashboard.

---

### Adsify (adsify.digital) — US-Focused Amazon Specialist

**What they are:** Amazon-only agency targeting US brands. SPN + Advanced Partner. $100M+ ad spend, 200+ brands.

**Their client profile:** US Amazon sellers scaling from $5K to $500K/month.

**Their real weakness:**
- 100% US-focused — Indian sellers are completely outside their scope
- Amazon-only — no Meta Ads, no Flipkart, no Myntra, no Meesho
- No cross-border expertise for India ↔ UK or India ↔ US sellers
- No India marketplace specialization whatsoever

**Their gap:** Any brand that sells on Indian marketplaces OR wants an India-based expert for UK Amazon.

---

### Nine58 (nine58.in) — No Significant Web Presence

Almost no searchable footprint. Likely a micro-agency or freelancer team. Not a strategic threat.
