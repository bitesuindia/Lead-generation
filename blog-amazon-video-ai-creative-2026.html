Amazon Video Ads in 2026: Winning with AI Creative, Video Generator & Sponsored TV

Ranjeet Saini · July 2, 2026 · 11 min read · Creative & Video
All Articles
    ← Back to Blog

    For years, the single biggest reason Amazon sellers skipped video advertising wasn't strategy — it was production. Video was slow, expensive, and technical, so most brands defaulted to static images and quietly ceded the most engaging ad formats to bigger competitors. In 2026, that excuse is gone. Amazon's Creative Agent and its free Video Generator have collapsed video production from weeks and thousands of dollars to minutes and, in many cases, no extra cost at all.

    That changes the maths for every seller. When professional-grade video becomes free to produce, the advertisers who win are no longer the ones with the biggest creative budgets — they're the ones who deploy video fastest and place it smartest across the funnel. This guide covers the new AI creative tools, why video is now table stakes, and exactly where to put it to work.

    1. The creative bottleneck just disappeared

    In February 2026, Amazon Ads launched Creative Agent, an agentic AI tool that produces professional-quality display and video ads from a simple chat. It's built on AWS Bedrock, using Amazon's Nova models and Anthropic's Claude, and it draws on Amazon's retail insights to understand your brand, product, and audience before it generates anything. Alongside it sits Video Generator, a free tool that turns a single product image or ASIN into finished video ads in minutes.

    Together, these tools remove the three barriers that kept most sellers out of video: cost, time, and skill. You no longer need a videographer, an editor, or a four-week timeline. You need a product, an idea, and a few minutes.

    The strategic implication is bigger than convenience. When every seller can produce video for free, video stops being a competitive advantage and becomes competitive parity — the new baseline. The edge shifts from "can you make video?" to "how well do you deploy it, and where?"

    2. Why video is now table stakes on Amazon

    Video isn't just more engaging — it's the key that unlocks Amazon's fastest-growing, highest-value ad inventory. Static images can't run on Streaming TV. They can't play in Prime Video. And they consistently under-index against video in dense, comparison-heavy placements where motion captures attention. The money is following that reality.

    3. Inside Creative Agent: the AI production pipeline

    What makes Creative Agent different from a generic AI video tool is that it runs the entire production pipeline conversationally, grounded in Amazon's own shopping signals. From a chat window inside Creative Studio, it researches your product and audience, brainstorms concepts, develops them into storyboards, then produces the finished asset — writing multi-scene scripts, generating images, animating scenes, and adding voiceover and music.

The practical workflow shift: your team spends its time on creative direction and testing, not production labour.

    4. Video Generator: free 15-second ads in minutes

    If Creative Agent is the full studio, Video Generator is the express lane. It takes a single product image or ASIN and produces up to six 15-second, multi-scene video ads — complete with text animation and background music — in minutes, at no additional cost, for Sponsored Brands video campaigns.

    For most sellers, this is the fastest possible on-ramp to video. Here's how we use it with the brands we manage:

    • Generate several variants per hero ASIN and let them run — with six versions from one image, you have a built-in creative test from day one.
    • Lead with the outcome in the first three seconds. Show the product solving a real problem before the viewer's attention drifts.
    • Refresh creative on a schedule to fight ad fatigue — something that was never economical when each video cost real money and time.
    • Feed winners into Creative Agent to build richer, longer-form versions for upper-funnel placements.

    5. Where to deploy video across the funnel

    Free production only pays off if the placement is deliberate. This is where most sellers will leave money on the table — making video, then dumping it all into one campaign type. Here's how we prioritise placements, from the highest-return, lowest-effort starting point upward.

    Start where intent is highest and effort is lowest — Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Products video — to bank quick, measurable wins. Then reinvest those returns into Sponsored TV and DSP to build a genuine full-funnel presence. Measure the whole thing on TaCoS and new-to-brand growth, not the ACoS of any single campaign, because the value of upper-funnel video shows up as lift across the account, not inside the line item that paid for it.

    One caution: free, fast creative makes it tempting to flood every placement at once. Don't. Scale into upper-funnel video only after your bottom-funnel video is profitable — otherwise you're buying reach you can't yet convert. Discipline is the difference between a full-funnel strategy and an expensive brand-awareness hobby.

    Frequently asked questions

    What is Amazon Creative Agent?

    It's Amazon Ads' agentic AI creative tool, launched in early 2026 and built on AWS Bedrock using Amazon Nova and Anthropic Claude. Through a chat interface in Creative Studio it researches your product and audience, brainstorms concepts, writes multi-scene scripts, and produces finished video and display ads for use across DSP, Streaming TV, Sponsored Display, and Sponsored Brands.

    Is Amazon's Video Generator really free?

    Yes. Video Generator produces up to six 15-second multi-scene video ads from a single product image or ASIN — with text animation and background music — in minutes, at no additional cost, for Sponsored Brands video campaigns.

    Do video ads actually convert better than images?

    Video typically earns higher engagement and click-through in competitive placements, and it unlocks inventory images can't reach, like Sponsored TV and Prime Video. The exact uplift varies by category and product, so test video against your own image benchmarks rather than assuming a fixed number.
